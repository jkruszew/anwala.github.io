Slopestyle - All you need to know for the Winter Olympics 2018 event

  • Slopestyle competition will take place at the Phoenix Snow Park
  • Winter Olympics schedule for slopestyle runs from 17th - 18th February
  • Slopestyle takes place on a course that includes a number of obstacles
  • Athletes perform twice, and the higher score will decide the final ranking

By Max Winters For Mailonline

Published: 14:35 EST, 7 February 2018 | Updated: 08:43 EST, 15 February 2018

  • e-mail

Introduced at the previous Winter Olympics held in Russian city Sochi in 2014, slopestyle takes place on a course that includes a number of obstacles such as rails, tables, boxes, walls and jumps. Athletes are able to pick which obstacles feature in their performance.

Judges mark each athlete's performance according to a number of different factors such as height, turn, technique, and difficulty on a full score of 100, out of which an average decides the ranking.

Where's the Pyeongchang 2018 venue for slopestyle?

Action will take place at the Phoenix Snow Park. The facility can house18,000 spectators with seating for 10,200. This venue will be the base for all freestyle skiing competitions throughout the Games.

How does slopestyle work in the Winter Games?

Athletes get two runs down the course and can pick their own line from the various obstacles on offer Their highest score will decide the final ranking. Through the qualifications, up to 24 men and 12 women can progress to the finals.

Winter Olympic records for slopestyle

International Olympic Committee does not consider slopestyle scores eligible for Olympic records.
