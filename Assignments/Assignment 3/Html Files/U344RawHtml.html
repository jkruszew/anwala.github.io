Mirai Nagasu lands a triple axel, the first American woman to do it at Winter Olympics

Nancy Armour, USA TODAY Sports Published 8:48 p.m. CT Feb. 11, 2018 | Updated 11:41 a.m. CT Feb. 12, 2018
CLOSE

Mirai Nagasu became the first American woman to land a triple axel at the Winter Olympics when she landed the jump in the team competition in Pyeongchang. USA TODAY Sports

Mirai Nagasu of the USA mid-air.(Photo: Robert Deutsch, USA TODAY Sports)

CONNECTTWEETLINKEDIN 1 COMMENTEMAILMORE

PYEONGCHANG, South Korea — Mirai Nagasu’s first performance at the Pyeongchang Olympics was a historic one.

Nagasu became the first American woman to land a triple axel at the Olympics on Monday, doing it during the team competition. It was the first jump in her program, and she landed it perfectly.

Tonya Harding and 2006 world champion Kimmie Meissner had landed the triple axel in competition, but neither had done it at the Olympics.

Japan’s Midori Ito and Mao Asada are the only other women to land the 3 ½-revolution jump at the Winter Games.
