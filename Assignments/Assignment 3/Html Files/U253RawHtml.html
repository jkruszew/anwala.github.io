Goals drying up on the pitch, persistent back problems and now that dancing video... the questions are adding up for Alvaro Morata

  • Alvaro Morata posted video spinning wife Alice Campello around in his arms 
  • Chelsea striker has been out with back problems and clip has brought criticism 
  • Striker says video is old but this is latest incident and questions are adding up
  • Spaniard has flirted with return to Madrid but declared himself fit for West Brom 

By Oliver Todd for MailOnline

Published: 08:02 EST, 12 February 2018 | Updated: 10:59 EST, 12 February 2018

  • e-mail
49 shares

When footage emerged of Alvaro Morata spinning around with his wife in his arms in their kitchen at home on Sunday night, there was an obvious question being asked by those watching:

What was a man facing possibly missing the rest of the season with a chronic back problem doing putting strain on the affected area — and then publishing video of it online?

Antonio Conte has admitted he is in the dark over how long Morata will be out for and, for a striker who hasn’t scored since Boxing Day, the questions are adding up.

Alice Campello (right) shared footage of herself dancing with her husband Alvaro Morata

Morata has been absent for a month and there are plenty of questions surrounding him

Chelsea’s £70.6million record signing has been out for almost a month and has been publicly courting a move back to Real Madrid. It is not a good look.

Now, the focus is on Morata to reignite Chelsea’s season with a potential return mooted, by the striker himself, against West Bromwich Albion on Monday night.

Morata first started suffering problems with his back as early as mid-December.

Chelsea were fresh from their Carabao Cup semi-final first leg against Arsenal with trips to West Ham and Huddersfield Town lined up for the coming days in the Premier League.

He started at the London Stadium after overcoming illness but flattered to deceive. Three days later he was unfit for the trip to the John Smith’s Stadium with what was reported as ‘tiredness’ — the fatigue of a first season as a leading No 9 was affecting him.

‘Morata is out, because he’s a bit tired and there is a problem in his back,’ Conte said.

‘I think when you play every three days to have a training session and to work on the physical aspects. It's impossible because you have to prepare with the players for a game every three days.’

Morata would only play 17 minutes of Chelsea’s next three matches. At the same time, wife Alice revealed she was pregnant. The couple are expecting twins.

Morata's wife is expecting twins and off the pitch there is plenty going on for the striker

The 25-year-old returned to the starting XI against Brighton on Boxing Day and scored, a header from Cesar Azpilicueta’s cross.

Chelsea were trying to manage the pain in Morata’s back with regular work on it at their Cobham training base, but the discomfort continued.

‘We are struggling a lot to find a solution to solve the pain in his back,’ Conte said recently.

Morata was wasteful with a host of chances against Stoke City at home and then famously missed a hat-trick of golden opportunities away at Arsenal.

He started two further games against Arsenal and Leicester City in January, with Chelsea failing to score in either.

When the Spaniard came on against Norwich City in an FA Cup third-round replay he was largely frustrated and then was sent off in extra-time for two bookings in quick succession.

He has not played since.

Antonio Conte admitted the club were struggling to find a solution to Morata's back pain

Morata’s work in dealing with his back injury has included visits to doctor Peter Wehling, a specialist who has worked with basketball’s Kobe Bryant in the past.

Chelsea have their striker in at their Cobham headquarters each day, trying to manage the injury and find a solution through physiotherapy.

Despite Conte’s protestations about the problem this week, Morata was always thought to be hopeful of a return in time to face Barcelona in the Champions League.

He has now stated he is fit to play against West Brom on Monday night.

Of the clip posted by his wife, Morata wrote: ‘Its old video. Im not injury im ok for the game tomorrow. A Lot os critics now.. but no problem for me! (sic)’.

Morata insists the video he posted dancing with Alice was old and he is fit to play again

The Spain striker also has also suffered personal problems of late. A close friend died in a car accident towards the end of last month and Chelsea offered him time off from his treatment after being informed of the bereavement.

The publication of an interview last week, talking of how ‘Madrid is Madrid’ — his love for his former home is still clear, was poorly timed.

He told Movistar+: ‘Obviously I have to respect Chelsea, who made a huge effort to sign me and I'm very happy here. But Madrid will always be Madrid for me.’

It is not the first time Morata has spoken of a desire to return to his homeland. In October he was speaking of how he didn’t seem himself in London ‘for very long’.

The situation for Chelsea’s record signing is a bleak one: goals drying up on the pitch, back problems keeping him off the field and personal problems blighting his private life. His team are struggling without him, too.

The Spanish international started the season well and remains a key player for Conte's side

‘We have been talking a lot about bad results but sometimes we forget we are facing a serious situation,’ Conte said last week.

‘Players like Morata, for us, are very important and have been missing for a long period of time. I don’t know how long he will be out.

‘If you ask me whether he needs a day, a month or the rest of the season, I don’t know. For this reason, I’m a bit worried. You know very well the importance of the player.’

Sunday’s release of the video footage, shared by his wife, only added to the scrutiny. But the clip, which also showed Morata drying Alice’s hair, was not taken recently.

He now says he is ready to return to action.

What is known now is that Morata needs to get the focus back on his football. Chelsea’s No 9 started this season well. He has 12 goals in 31 appearances, a respectable total for a first campaign in England.

But there is a lot to prove. Morata will hope the months of December and January can be forgotten when he gets back to fitness — whenever that is. 

Morata still has plenty to prove at Chelsea and could yet finish the season strongly
