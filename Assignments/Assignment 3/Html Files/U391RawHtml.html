Eagles' Lane Johnson calls Patriots 'fear-based organization'

Steve Gardner, USA TODAY Sports Published 8:27 p.m. ET Feb. 11, 2018
CLOSE

SportsPulse: Weren't able to make it to the Eagles' epic Super Bowl parade? Get a look inside of the wild crowd that was overjoyed to celebrate one of the biggest days in Philadelphia sports history. USA TODAY Sports

Eagles offensive tackle Lane Johnson is rarely afraid to say what's on his mind.(Photo: Kirby Lee, USA TODAY Sports)

CONNECTTWEETLINKEDIN 9 COMMENTEMAILMORE

For outspoken Philadelphia Eagles offensive lineman Lane Johnson, the animosity toward the New England Patriots didn't end with his team's victory in the Super Bowl.

In a recent interview, Johnson called the Patriots a "fear-based organization" that doesn't allow its players to have fun playing the game.

Johnson, who drew attention in the build-up to Super Bowl LII when he called New England quarterback Tom Brady a "pretty boy," made his latest comments on Barstool Sports' "Pardon My Take" podcast.

"Obviously, do they win? Hell yes, they win. They've won for a long time," Johnson said. "Do I think people enjoy and can say, 'I had a lot of fun playing there?' No, I don't. That's just the God's honest truth."

More: San Francisco 49ers linebacker Reuben Foster arrested

Johnson also criticized Patriots players for acting like "robots" when dealing with the media during Super Bowl week. 

"You only get to do this job one time, so let's have fun while we're doing it," he said. "Not to be reckless, but I'd much rather have fun and win a Super Bowl than be miserable and win five Super Bowls."

Johnson also expressed optimism the Eagles can repeat as Super Bowl champions. 

"I don't want to have that relief factor. I feel like a lot of teams once they start getting good (say), 'Now we can set and relax.'

"We need to push the pedal," Johnson said. "I think now is our year to shine."
