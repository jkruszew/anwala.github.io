Alvaro Morata's wife Alice Campello posts video of Chelsea star dancing after Antonio Conte says back injury could keep him 'out for the season'

  • Antonio Conte appeared concerned about Alvaro Morata's back injury on Friday
  • Boss Conte said: 'Maybe he needs one day, one month, or is out for the season'
  • On Sunday, Morata's wife shared a video of the Chelsea striker dancing with her
  • Morata claimed the footage was old and declared himself now fit to play

By Robert Summerscales For Mailonline

Published: 17:34 EST, 11 February 2018 | Updated: 07:46 EST, 12 February 2018

Chelsea manager Antonio Conte claimed on Friday that Alvaro Morata may not play again this season due to his niggling back injury but he appeared in decent shape in a video shared by his wife Alice Campello on Sunday.

The footage posted on Instagram showed Morata twirling and then lifting his wife, who is pregnant with their first child.

The former Real Madrid frontman has missed Chelsea's last five games, with Conte revealing concerns that his £75million buy could be out for the long term. 

Alice Campello (right) has shared footage of herself dancing with her husband Alvaro Morata

It is unclear how recently the video was shot but Morata has missed Chelsea's last five games

Spanish ace Morata, who has been sidelined with a back injury, was seen lifting his Italian wife

Morata wrapped his arms around his wife, who shared the video with her Instagram followers

Many fans voiced their disapproval after seeing the video, complaining that Morata should play for Chelsea if he was fit enough to dance.

Morata has since declared himself fit to play for Chelsea against West Bromwich Albion on Monday night — despite Conte's worries.

Morata has since commented on the clip, writing: 'Its old video. Im not injury im ok for the game tomorrow. A Lot of critics now.. but no problem for me! (sic)'

It is unclear when the clip was filmed, but one supporter commented: 'He's supposed to have a bad back!!!!'

Another wrote: 'He should play instead of dancing.'

Chelsea have struggled in Morata's absence, losing three of their last five games.

Prior to his latest spell on the sidelines, the 25-year-old had netted 10 goals in 20 Premier League appearances.

Conte stopped well short of categorically ruling Morata out for the remainder of the campaign when he spoke to the media on Friday, but he was clearly very concerned.

He said: 'We talk a lot about bad results, but we are facing a serious situation, with players like Morata who are important, that are missing for a long time.

'He has this problem, we are trying to find a solution to solve the pain in his back. 

'You ask me how long he is out, maybe he needs one day, one month, or is out for the season, I don't know. I am a bit worried as we know the importance of the player.'
