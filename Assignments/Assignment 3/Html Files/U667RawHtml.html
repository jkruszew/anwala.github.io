      1. What is the minimum age?


      Amateur Race 15 years
      Green Ride 12 years


      2. What is the reporting time for the event?


      This will depend on the ride category that you have signed up for and its allocated time as mentioned on the website.


      3. How do I register?


      You can register online at www.sakshamcyclothon.com


      4. What are the registration fees?


      Amateur Race 700
      Green Ride Free


      5. Can I ride with somebody elses Bib number?


      No, you Cannot


      6. What if I register but cannot take part?


      If you cannot take part, you may find another cyclist to take your place for the event as long as the organizers are informed of this substitution in advance. Substitution is at the discretion of the Event Organizers.


      7. What do I wear?


      Cycling shoes, shorts and jerseys are recommended for your safety and convenience, but you can wear what you like. Helmets are compulsory.


      8. What kind of bicycle do you recommend?


      It is compulsory that you have either a MTB/ Hybrid bike or a ROAD bike, depending on the race category you have registered for.


      9. What do I do if I have to get a bicycle on rent?


      Cycles will be made available at the Venue Rental Bays on the day of the event. Do note the rentals are subject to availability and will be at an additional cost. Upon completion of the payment process, you would receive your unique number/ ticket/ sticker on the BiB itself to be presented at the Rental Bay at the event venue. Upon presenting the unique number/ ticket/ sticker, a cycle shall be issued to you only for the Race Day against a valid ID proof (Passport, Aadhar Card, Driving License) that will be kept in our possession. Only after returning the cycle at the Rental Bay, you would receive the ID proof back.


      10. How can I confirm the status of my entry?


      After you register online you will receive an e-mail confirming your entry.


      11. Where do I collect my bib number?


      Details of venue and Bib collection would be shared on email closer to the event day, so please look out for emailers from the event.


      12. What route does the event follow?


      Route map shall be available soon on the website.


      13. What precautions are being taken regarding traffic on the day of the event?


      All involved roads will be closed for the duration of the event by the organizers and the respective authorities.


      14. What if I decide during the ride that I cannot continue?


      There is a sweep vehicle that follows the route. Move of the course and wait for the sweep vehicle or you can stop at any of the official pitstops.


      15. What happens to me if I am in an accident?


      If you are in an accident on the route, our medical team will attend to you. If you are able to, you can continue to the finish line or you can wait to be picked up by the sweep vehicle. If you are more seriously injured, you will be attended to by our paramedics and taken to the nearest hospital. It is therefore very important to ensure that your medical and emergency details are correctly updated and that you ride with your own bib number.


      16. If I am in an accident and have to go to the hospital in an ambulance, what happens to my bicycle?


      Your bicycle will be picked up by the sweeper vehicle and will be taken to event venue from where you can arrange for it to be taken back home.


      17. Will there be a baggage counter to keep my personal belongings?


      Yes there will be a provision for the same.

    Saksham Cyclothon is Indias Premier Cyclothon, which aims to propagate the importance of fuel conservation and efficient energy utilization, amongst the citizens of our Nation.

    In an effort to instill socio-environmental consciousness, Saksham Cyclothon is an initiative by Petroleum Conservation Research Association (PCRA), a non-profit organization setup in 1978 under the aegis of Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas, Government of India, engaged in promoting energy efficiency in various sections of the economy. Expected to witness about 5000 participants.
